## Mao Zedong’s “Report on an Investigation of the Hunan Peasant Movement (Week 10)

## Long March, 1934-35 and the establishment of the CCP base at Yan’an (Week 10)
Mao Zedong joins up with the warlord officer Zhu De
in the Jiangxi area to create a base of resistance
• 1931 the Jiangxi Soviet Republic becomes the
ideological center of the CCP
• Meanwhile, Chiang Kai-shek’s Nationalist forces try
to exterminate Communist base; Communist forces
excel at guerrilla warfare
• 1934: Chiang’s forces, with the help of German
military advisers, dislodge the Jiangxi Soviet, forcing
the CCP forces of perhaps 100,000 to retreat
• The legendary Long March: nearly 6000 miles in a
year, averaging 17 miles a day, with only 4000-8000
surviving the entirety of the ordeal

## Cultural Revolution, 1966-1976 (Week 10)

Mao Zedong increasingly upset by revisionism within the party

• Decides to retake control of the party and initiate a new revolution, a Great Revolution to Create a Proletarian Culture

• Organizes ouster of several powerful party officials, claiming that they were anti-Mao and “capitalistroaders”

• Makes appeals directly to young people, particularly students, deputizing them as “Red Guards” and fanning the flames of anti-establishment sentiment

• Many Red Guards travel to Beijing to participate in gigantic mass rallies

• “Destroy the Four Old Things” (customs, habits, culture, and thinking)

• Tight control of culture

## Tokyo War Crimes Trials (Week 11)

Demilitarize Japan

• Prosecute war crimes through the International Military Tribunal for the Far East (Tokyo War Crimes Trials)

• 28 “Class A” prisoners charged with “crimes against peace” (7 convicted and sentenced to hanging); another 4,405 military personnel convicted of “Class C” crimes: war crimes like mistreatment of prisoners

• Hirohito: absolved of serious responsibility

• Constitution Article 9: “The Japanese people forever renounce war as a sovereign right of the nation. . . Land, sea, and air forces will never be maintained.”

## Japan’s revised constitution of 1946 (Week 11)

November 1946, amended constitution: reaffirm representative democracy

• Women gain right to vote

• Diet’s Lower House given more power; Upper Housemembers to be elected (rather than hereditary or awarded by government)

• Freedom of assembly, speech, religion, unionization and collective bargaining; freedom from unlawful arrest

• Redistribution of farmland to reduce tenant farming and absentee landlordism

• Suppression of Japanese Communist Party (JCP) and Japanese Socialist Party (JSP) members

• Follow America’s lead with foreign relations
